# P2.2 — Serializer and clean-output boundaries

## Decision (PROPOSED)

> This record is **proposed, not binding**. Per P0.1 §4 it carries no authority
> until the owner (`github:bart-turczynski`) merges it into `main`; the merge is
> the ratifying act and the seal in §Ratification stamps `ACCEPTED`. Until then
> its `state` is `PROPOSED` and every dependent artifact treats it as a proposal.

RCON-03 (reconciliation §4) is that source reproduction, standards
serialization, cleaning, and safe display are **conflated** into one shipped
surface — the `clean_url` product string and the `.serialize_*_vec()` helpers
that build it. This record separates those output surfaces, gives each its own
contract, disposes contradictions **C-04**, **C-05**, and the **serialization
half of C-12**, and names the full-string standard-serialization surface that
P5.3's conformance/oracle policy must stand on.

### 1. Four distinct v3 output surfaces (separates RCON-03's conflation)

v3 recognizes **four** distinct output surfaces. Each has an input-state
requirement, an exact output, a delimiter/credential policy, and a fixed set of
allowed downstream uses. No surface may silently stand in for another. (A fifth
surface — the non-URL **comparison key** — is named in RCON-03 but is owned by
tier P3, not here; it is listed only to keep the boundary complete.)

| # | Surface | v3 name (working) | Shipped mapping today | Fragment | Credentials | Round-trips to source | Spec-exact |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| a | **Source reproduction** | `url_source()` | `original_url` result field (`R/utils.R:216`) | verbatim | verbatim | yes (bytes, unmutated row) | n/a (echoes input) |
| b | **Standard serialization** | **`serialize_url(x, standard=)`** | *unimplemented* (no shipped entry point) | **preserved** | **reconstructed** | via parse→serialize→parse identity | **yes** (WHATWG / RFC 3986) |
| c | **Clean output** | `clean_url` / `get_clean_url()` | `clean_url` field; `.build_clean_url_vec()`, `.serialize_whatwg_vec()`, `.serialize_rfc_generic_vec()` (`R/parse-phases.R:2375,2445,2548`) | **omitted** | **not reconstructed** | no (intentionally lossy) | no (SEO/policy product) |
| d | **Safe display** | `format_url()` | *unimplemented* (no shipped entry point) | policy | redacted/annotated | no | no (human-readable) |

**a. Source reproduction.** Faithful reproduction of the accepted input for an
unmutated row. Today this is the `original_url` field: it retains the supplied
string bytes; non-ASCII returned values are declared UTF-8, so byte preservation
and R `Encoding()`-label preservation are already **different** guarantees (S3
§S3-F1; `R/parse.R:765-805`). Whether v3 promises source-byte reproduction, the
`Encoding()` label, both, or a bounded subset is an open owner question (Open
Q4); this record only fixes that source reproduction is its **own** surface,
never a serializer.

**b. Standard serialization — the full-string surface (FSSS).** A pair of
**spec-exact** serializers, one per standard, that emit the standard's own
serialization of the parsed state: **WHATWG** (`#concept-url-serializer`) and
**RFC 3986** (§5.3 component recomposition). This surface is **full-string,
fragment-complete, and credential-complete**: it emits userinfo (with delimiter
state), a trailing `?`/`#` when the delimiter was present with an empty value
(ADR 0012 D2), and the fragment. It consumes the **lossless serializer-input
record** (S3 §S3-F2's mandated artifact — `authority_kind`, `host_kind`,
credential/delimiter state, `query_kind`, `fragment_kind`, path posture), never
the 18-field public projection and never a cleaned/formatted component. Its
correctness oracle is parse→serialize→parse structural equivalence plus
full-string fixtures. **This is the named surface P5.3 depends on** — see §4.

**c. Clean output.** The **legacy** `clean_url` field and `get_clean_url()`
accessor: an intentionally lossy, policy-driven SEO/canonicalization product
(strip www, force scheme, trailing-slash/index policy, tracking-param removal;
`R/accessors.R:228-256`). By longstanding product contract it **omits the
fragment and does not reconstruct credentials** (verified: `R/parse-phases.R`
comment lines 2432-2436 — "the FRAGMENT is EXCLUDED"; the `.serialize_*_vec()`
signatures take no fragment/username/password argument, `R/parse-phases.R:2445`).
This surface stays byte-for-byte compatible for the CRAN default (D4 hard
constraint) and remains lossy **on purpose**.

**d. Safe display.** A human-readable rendering with hazard handling (controls,
bidi overrides, invisibles, spoof-safe host display). No shipped implementation
exists (S3 §S3-F3; `format_url()` is proposed-only). Its exact escape/annotation
matrix is out of scope for P2.2 and deferred (Open Q3); this record only fixes
that display is a **separate** surface whose output is **never** fed into
serialization, mutation baselines, or keys.

### 2. C-04 — fragment-preserving standard serialization is UNIMPLEMENTED, not superseded

Contradiction C-04 (reconciliation §5): ADR 0012 requires fragment-preserving
standard serialization (D2: standard serializers "retain a trailing `?` or `#`",
ADR 0012:262-268) while the shipped clean serializers explicitly omit fragments
(`R/parse-phases.R:2432-2436`).

**Disposition — SEPARATE, and the ADR 0012 standard serializer is UNIMPLEMENTED
(to be built), NOT superseded.** The shipped `.serialize_whatwg_vec()` /
`.serialize_rfc_generic_vec()` are **clean-output** serializers (surface c) that
implement the `clean_url` product contract; they are *not* the ADR 0012 standard
serializer and were never meant to be. There is no conflict once the surfaces are
distinguished: the **clean** surface may keep dropping fragments as a deliberate
cleaning choice, and the **standard** serialization surface (b) **must not** —
it preserves fragments and credentials. ADR 0012 D2 remains accepted and its
standard serializer is scheduled work (ADR 0012 Layer 3b), not a superseded idea.
This matches S3's blocker correction: "V3's proposed separate standards
serializer should not silently inherit `clean_url`'s omission" (S3 §S3-C1).

### 3. C-05 — `%2F` preservation is a clean/compat dial, kept OUT of the standard serializer

Contradiction C-05 (reconciliation §5): ADR 0011 describes WHATWG `path_encoding`
as lossy for encoded `/` (collapses reserved `%2F`), while shipped WHATWG
`encode` preserves `%2F` (S3 §S3-F6; `R/path-query.R:206-269`).

**Disposition — legacy compatibility PRESERVED, dial kept out of the standards
serializer contract.** `%2F` handling is governed by the `path_encoding`
presentation axis, which ADR 0011 already established as an **orthogonal
presentation knob, not a governed identity axis**. The shipped WHATWG `encode`
behavior (which preserves existing `%xx` spellings, so `%2F` survives) is
**retained** for byte-compatibility of the clean surface (surface c) — no working
call changes output. Per ADR 0011 and ADR 0012 D2 the lossy `path_encoding`
values "never feed the standard serializer used for identity comparisons"; this
record **binds** that rule: `path_encoding` (and every `%2F`/reserved-octet
compat dial) is a **clean/display presentation dial only**. The standard
serialization surface (b) renders the path from the internal identity record per
the selected standard's own percent-encode set and **never consults
`path_encoding`**. The apparent ADR 0011-vs-code contradiction is thereby a
surface-assignment question, not a behavior change: ADR 0011's "lossy" prose
describes the clean/presentation path; the code's `%2F` preservation is that same
presentation surface's compat behavior.

### 4. C-12 (serialization half) — define the full-string surface conformance rebuilds from

Contradiction C-12 (reconciliation §5) / finding RCON-10: historical drafts make
strong conformance claims from **component projections** while acknowledging the
missing **faithful full-string serialization** surface.

**Disposition (serialization half only).** The v3 conformance substrate is the
**full-string standard-serialization surface** named in §1(b) —
`serialize_url(x, standard = c("whatwg", "rfc3986"))`, hereafter the **FSSS**.
Conformance for a given standard is asserted against the **FSSS output string**
(and parse→serialize→parse identity), never against the 18-field component
projection. Component fields (`scheme`, `host`, `path`, `query`, `fragment`, …)
are a lossy view: they collapse empty-vs-absent to `NA`, drop delimiter state,
and are shaped by presentation dials — they are **historical evidence only**, not
a conformance oracle. This record delivers **only the serialization surface** on
which claims stand. **The claim/oracle policy itself** — how conformance is
claimed, that projections are historical evidence only, oracle authority,
metamorphic fixtures, and the parity-ledger-vs-oracle distinction — is the scope
of sibling record **P5.3** (oracle/claim policy), which **depends on this
record's FSSS definition**. P2.2 fixes *what the full string is*; P5.3 fixes
*how a claim is made against it*.

### 5. Surface-assignment invariants (binding once accepted)

1. **No surface substitutes for another.** The clean surface (c) is never used as
   a serializer, redirect target, comparison identity, or conformance oracle; the
   standard surface (b) is never used as a human display; display (d) output never
   re-enters serialization/mutation/keys (S3 §S3-F3).
2. **Standard serializers consume the lossless serializer-input record**, never
   the public projection or a cleaned/formatted component (S3 §S3-F2).
3. **Presentation dials are clean/display-only.** `path_encoding`, `host_encoding`
   (unicode/idna presentation), `query_handling` filtering/sorting, and the
   cleaning dials feed surfaces (c)/(d) only, never surface (b).
4. **Fragment and credentials are serialized by (b), dropped by (c).** This is a
   per-surface contract, not a global URL policy.
5. **Credential-output policy governs *all* preserved credentials — the P4.1
   (B9) cross-reference.** This surface split is the secret-in-output guard that
   P4.1 (C-11) relies on when it preserves parsed **generic-authority** (non-web)
   userinfo in canonical state. Preservation in the canonical record is safe
   precisely because output is surface-governed here: surface (c) `clean_url`
   **never** reconstructs credentials, surface (d) `format_url()` **redacts** them,
   and **only** the deliberate surfaces (a) source reproduction and (b) exact
   standard serialization may reproduce them — for web *and* generic-authority
   credentials alike. Owner-ratified with P4.1 at the 2026-07-23 review (B9).

## Rationale

- **RCON-03 (reconciliation §4/§125-153)** requires "five separate output
  contracts, each with an input-state requirement, exact output type, delimiter
  and credential policy, encoding policy, safety properties, reparse guarantee,
  loss report, and allowed downstream uses" and "full-string serializer fixtures
  rather than component projections alone." §1 delivers the four surfaces in
  P2.2's remit (the fifth, comparison keys, is deferred to P3 by the queue in
  reconciliation §8).
- **S3 evidence (frozen, `evidence/S3-serialization-format-encoding.md`).** The
  two blocker corrections drive §1: S3-F1 ("'lossless or faithful' conflates
  source reproduction with standard serialization" → separate `url_source` and
  `serialize_url`) and S3-F2 ("the protocol does not require a serializer-ready
  lossless state record" → surface (b) consumes that record, not the projection).
  S3's contradiction table seeds C-04 (S3-C1), C-05 (S3-C2/S3-F6), and the
  clean-vs-serialization split (S3-C4). The credential-reassembly finding (S3-F5)
  is why surface (b) is credential-complete while (c) is not.
- **ADR 0012 D2 (accepted; `design/adr/0012-general-url-parser-scope.md:262-268,
  275-283`)** requires standard serializers to retain empty-but-present `?`/`#`
  and states the lossy presentation path "never feeds the standard serializer
  used for identity comparisons." §2 and §3 apply that ADR precisely rather than
  superseding it.
- **ADR 0011 (accepted; `design/adr/0011-path-encoding-orthogonal-presentation.md`)**
  already classifies `path_encoding` as an orthogonal **presentation** knob whose
  `encode`/`decode` values are lossy on reserved octets and "not guaranteed to
  survive" as identity. §3 binds that classification to the clean/display
  surfaces and excludes it from the standard serializer.
- **ADR 0003 (parse/present Stage split)** underwrites the boundary: serialization
  from the internal identity record is a present-stage operation distinct from the
  presentation-dial Stage-B rendering that produces `clean_url`.
- **Verified shipped behavior (baseline 89be90b).** `.serialize_whatwg_vec()` /
  `.serialize_rfc_generic_vec()` take no fragment/credential arguments and carry
  the comment "the FRAGMENT is EXCLUDED" (`R/parse-phases.R:2432-2436,2445-2447`);
  `get_clean_url()` "omits fragment and does not reconstruct credentials" per the
  public-surface inventory (`registers/public-surface-inventory.md`, `clean_url`
  and `get_clean_url` rows, citing §10 and S3). This confirms the shipped
  serializers are the clean surface, not a standard serializer — the premise of
  §2's disposition.

## Invalidated artifacts

None. This record is additive: it separates surfaces without altering shipped
behavior. The shipped `clean_url` / `get_clean_url` output is unchanged (surface
c stays byte-compatible, D4); the standard serializer (surface b) and safe
display (surface d) are new, unbuilt surfaces. No accepted ADR is superseded — 
ADR 0012 D2 and ADR 0011 are **applied**, not overturned. No prior decision
record depends on the conflated behavior, so nothing is marked `INVALIDATED`.

## Consequences

- **ADR 0012's standard serializer (Layer 3b) is confirmed as scheduled,
  unbuilt work.** Building surface (b) requires the lossless serializer-input
  record (S3-F2) first, which is P1/RCON-02 canonical-state work — so surface (b)
  cannot be implemented before the state contract lands. This record fixes the
  contract and the name, not the implementation.
- **`clean_url` is now explicitly the clean surface, not a serializer.** Downstream
  code that (mis)uses `clean_url` as an identity/redirect/serialization value —
  notably `canonical_join()` keying on `clean_url` (S3-F6; RCON-07) — is legacy
  behavior to be migrated under P3, not evidence that the clean surface is a
  serializer. This record does not itself change the join; it removes the excuse.
- **P5.3 is unblocked on the serialization axis.** It can name `serialize_url(x,
  standard=)` / the FSSS as the substrate its conformance claims are asserted
  against, and treat component projections as historical evidence only.
- **G2.A (contradiction closure) advances** for C-04 and C-05 (fully disposed
  here) and for the serialization half of C-12; **G3 (contract-matrix closure)**
  gains the output-contract cells for the four surfaces (the fifth, keys, is a P3
  cell).
- **New surface to build and verify** — a full-string WHATWG serializer, a
  full-string RFC 3986 recomposer, and (later) `format_url()` — each needing
  full-string + parse→serialize→parse fixtures (G4). The clean serializers stay
  as-is for the clean surface.

## Open questions for the owner

1. **Surface (b) public entry-point name.** `serialize_url(x, standard=)` is the
   working name (S3-F1). Alternatives: a `standard=` argument on an existing
   accessor, or split `serialize_whatwg()` / `serialize_rfc()` functions.
   *Recommendation:* a single `serialize_url(x, standard = c("whatwg",
   "rfc3986"))` — one surface, standard as an argument, mirroring the
   `url_standard` selector vocabulary. **Please confirm the name.**
2. **RFC serializer posture.** ADR 0012 D1 distinguishes source-preserving
   `rfc-syntax` from the historically normalized direct `url_standard="rfc3986"`
   (S3-C3). Does surface (b)'s RFC variant serialize the `rfc-syntax`
   (source-preserving) form, the normalized form, or expose both?
   *Recommendation:* serialize the **parsed identity per the record's posture**
   (so `rfc-syntax` rows round-trip source spelling, normalized rows serialize
   the normalized form), with the posture carried in the serializer-input record.
3. **Safe-display (surface d) scope in v3.** Is `format_url()` in scope for the
   3.0 line, or deferred? Its escape/annotation matrix (S3-F3) is substantial.
   *Recommendation:* fix the surface boundary now (done here); defer the
   `format_url()` matrix to a dedicated P-tier record so P2.2 stays serializer-
   focused.
4. **Source-reproduction (surface a) guarantee.** Byte reproduction, R
   `Encoding()`-label reproduction, both, or a bounded subset (S3-F1/S3-C7)?
   *Recommendation:* promise **source-byte** reproduction for unmutated rows and
   document the `Encoding()`-label as UTF-8-normalized (matching shipped
   `original_url`), deferring any stronger label guarantee.
5. **C-12 half-split acceptance.** Confirm that disposing only the
   **serialization** half here and transferring the **claim/oracle** half to P5.3
   is the intended factoring (this record and P5.3 jointly close C-12).

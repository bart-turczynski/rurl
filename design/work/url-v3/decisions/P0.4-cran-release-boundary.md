# P0.4 — CRAN hold scope and exact v2/v3 release boundary

## Decision (PROPOSED)

> This record is the agent's **proposed** disposition of C-10, offered for the
> owner to ratify, reject, or adjust. Nothing here is binding until the owner
> merges it (P0.1 §4). Where a fact turned on the owner's intent rather than the
> repository state, it is flagged as an open question below.

**Recommended disposition: the two conflicting claims are reconciled by
*release line*, and neither is rejected.** They are not in genuine
contradiction once each is bound to the version line it governs.

1. **Protocol claim — "submit no package to CRAN until the curl-free 3.0 slice."**
   Proposed type against the §5 vocabulary: **ACCEPTED, scope-narrowed.** The
   curl-free-before-CRAN prohibition is retained, but its authority is bound
   **specifically to the v3 (curl-free) line** — it is the release gate for
   `3.0.0` and any later curl-free release. It does **not** reach back to freeze
   the v2.x maintenance line. This is the "name the affected release line"
   correction RCON-10 requires (§4 RCON-10; §5 C-10 "record the owner decision").

2. **Accepted tracker/epic claim — "v2.7 may be released after its determinism
   gate is met."** Proposed type: **ACCEPTED, COMPATIBILITY-ONLY (v2.x
   maintenance line).** The `RURL-gxqdmpcp` disposition — v2.7 determinism
   requirement met on 2026-07-20, curl removal (Tier 2) deferred, lifting the
   v2.7 CRAN hold left as an owner decision — is **not superseded** by the
   protocol. v2.7 (and any further v2.x maintenance release) remains a
   curl-bearing release governed by its **own** determinism gate, on the v2.x
   line, outside the v3 curl-free hold.

### The v2/v3 boundary (concrete)

- **v2.x = the maintenance line.** Still `Imports: curl` (verified in
  `DESCRIPTION`). Governed by the libcurl-determinism disposition of
  `RURL-gxqdmpcp`, not by the v3 curl-free hold. Its releasability turns on the
  determinism gate, which the epic records as met for 2.7.
- **v3.0 = the curl-free line.** The protocol's curl-free-before-CRAN
  prohibition governs this line. Before **any** v3 CRAN submission, the line
  must pass the **RCON-09 curl-removal closure gate**: zero `curl` reference in
  `DESCRIPTION`, `NAMESPACE`, generated help imports, load-time namespace
  requirements, `R/` runtime/indirect calls, and tests; a clean-library
  install/load/examples/vignettes/tests/`R CMD check` with `curl` absent; and a
  static scan reduced to an allowlisted set of historical/reference tools
  (S8 "Curl-free closure the protocol must require"; RCON-09 §4).

### Standing-mandate check (correctness beats back-compat)

The owner's standing mandate is that standards-exactness / correctness beats
preserving old behavior. Applied here, back-compat must not **silently widen**
the hold (freezing the shipping v2.x line to protect a v3 rule it was never
scoped to) nor **silently narrow** it (letting a v3 release ship while `curl` is
still present). The disposition keeps the hold exactly as wide as its curl-free
justification: v3 line held to the curl-free gate; v2.x line governed only by
its own determinism gate.

### Current release state (verified, not assumed)

- Released tags stop at **`v2.2.1`** (`git tag -l`): there is **no `v2.3`–`v2.7`
  tag**. `DESCRIPTION` declares `Version: 2.7.0` and `NEWS.md` carries a
  `## rurl 2.7.0` section, so **2.7.0 is prepared but unreleased** — i.e. it is
  the *held* v2.x release, consistent with the epic's open "lift the v2.7 hold"
  decision. This record does **not** itself lift that hold (that is the separate
  `RURL-gxqdmpcp` release act); it only establishes that the v3 curl-free hold
  does not block it.

### Open questions surfaced for the owner (residual product choices)

- **O1 — exact cutover version.** Is the curl-free line necessarily numbered
  **3.0.0**, or could a curl-free release land on a `2.x`/other number? The
  protocol's language is "curl-free 3.0", which this record reads literally as
  `3.0.0`, but the *binding* condition is "curl-free", not the digit `3`. The
  owner should confirm whether the gate attaches to the number `3.0.0` or to the
  curl-free property regardless of version number.
- **O2 — is v2.7 eligible now?** Whether to actually lift the v2.7 CRAN hold and
  submit is a `RURL-gxqdmpcp` release decision, not settled here. This record's
  claim is only that the *v3* hold does not bind v2.7.
- **O3 — further v2.x maintenance releases.** Whether any `v2.8`+ curl-bearing
  maintenance release is permitted before v3, or whether v2.7 is intended as the
  last v2.x, is left to the owner.

## Rationale

- **§5 C-10** states the contradiction and its required disposition verbatim:
  "The protocol prohibits CRAN release until curl-free v3, while accepted
  tracker history permits a v2.7 release after its determinism gate — Name the
  release line governed by the hold and record the owner decision." This record
  supplies exactly that name (v3 line) plus the reconciliation.
- **§4 RCON-10** is the finding C-10 stems from: conformance/determinism/
  migration/release claims are not executable, and specifically "The stated CRAN
  prohibition also conflicts with the latest accepted release disposition unless
  its target version is clarified." The required amendment calls for "an
  owner-approved release rule that names the affected release line" — the
  operative act of this record.
- **§8 P0** lists "CRAN hold scope and exact v2/v3 release boundary" as an
  unanswered P0 owner decision; this is that decision.
- **§10 direct-evidence table** independently verifies (a) the determinism
  workflow "is not currently a divergence gate" and (b) "curl removal needs more
  than replacing the parser call … metadata imports plus parsing, path/query,
  accessor, email, and test references." Both ground the curl-free gate that the
  v3 line — and only the v3 line — must pass.
- **S8 finding — Blocker 1** ("The CRAN rule conflicts with the accepted epic's
  latest release disposition", `S8-performance-curl-conformance-migration.md`
  §"Severity-ordered findings", and the "v2.7 versus v3 CRAN hold" row in
  §"Contradictions and open owner decisions"): the protocol twice requires
  curl-free 3.0 before CRAN (`protocol :204`, `:317`); `fp context
  RURL-gxqdmpcp` records the 2026-07-20 result that the UTF-8/C full suite and
  all 17 determinism dumps were clean, leaves lifting the **2.7** hold to the
  owner, and defers Tier 2 / curl removal. S8's required correction is to "Add a
  visible owner-decision row: whether v2.7 may be submitted after its recorded
  determinism gate, and whether the curl-free rule applies specifically to 3.0.
  Cite the decision and mark the superseded statement; do not infer it during
  drafting." This record transfers that substance: the curl-free rule applies
  **specifically to the v3 line**, and v2.7 eligibility is a separate v2.x-line
  determinism-gate decision. S8 labels this a "Design fork; blocker" — resolving
  it is precisely a product-owner act, which is why this record is `type:
  product` and awaits owner ratification.
- **Verified repo state:** `git tag -l` → released tags end at `v2.2.1` (no
  `v2.3`–`v2.7`); `DESCRIPTION` → `Version: 2.7.0` with `curl` still in
  `Imports`; `NEWS.md` → prepared `## rurl 2.7.0` section;
  `.github/workflows/determinism-probe.yml` header → "EVIDENCE COLLECTION, NOT A
  GATE … a divergence must NEVER fail the build." These confirm 2.7.0 is a
  prepared-but-held curl-bearing v2.x release and that the determinism probe is
  the (non-gating) evidence mechanism the epic's determinism decision rode on.

# P5.3 — Oracle and claim policy

## Relationship to P2.2

C-12 is disposed jointly by two sibling records. **P2.2** (Serializer and
clean-output boundaries, on this branch) owns the **serialization half**: it
defines the four v3 output surfaces and names the **full-string
standard-serialization surface (FSSS)** — `serialize_url(x, standard =
c("whatwg", "rfc3986"))` — a full-string, fragment-complete, credential-complete
serializer that consumes the lossless serializer-input record and whose
correctness oracle is parse→serialize→parse structural equivalence plus
full-string fixtures (P2.2 §1(b), §4). P2.2 fixes *what the full string is*.

**P5.3 owns the claim/oracle half**: *how a conformance claim is made against
that substrate*. This record treats P2.2's FSSS as **fixed and given** — it
re-decides no output surface and no serializer behavior. It adds three policies
on top of the FSSS: (1) a **claim policy** binding every v3 conformance claim to
the FSSS full string (and parse→serialize→parse identity) and forbidding the
component projection as a live oracle; (2) an **oracle taxonomy** in which every
oracle carries an explicit authority and claim boundary; and (3) a
**claim-provenance requirement** giving each normative claim a labeled oracle
plus positive-and-negative fixtures. Together, P2.2 (substrate) + P5.3
(claim/oracle policy) close C-12; the contradiction register already binds C-12
to both records.

### 1. Claim policy — conformance is claimed against the FSSS, never the projection

1. **What a v3 conformance claim IS.** A v3 conformance claim is an assertion,
   scoped to **one named standard** (`whatwg` or `rfc3986`) and **one labeled
   oracle** (§2), that a defined set of inputs produces the standard's own
   serialization. It is asserted against **exactly two substrates and no other**:

   - the **FSSS full-string output** — `serialize_url(x, standard = …)` (P2.2
     §1(b)/§4), full-string, fragment-complete, credential-complete; and
   - **parse→serialize→parse structural equivalence** over the lossless
     serializer-input record (P2.2's correctness oracle).

   Every normative conformance claim in v3 states its standard, its oracle
   label, its input/fixture set, and its comparison mode (exact full-string
   equality, or structural round-trip equivalence). A claim with none of these is
   not a v3 conformance claim.

2. **The boundary a claim may not cross.** No v3 conformance claim may be
   asserted against the **18-field public component projection** (`scheme`,
   `host`, `path`, `query`, `fragment`, `user`, `password`, `port`, …; verified
   18 public fields vs 20 Stage-A fields, reconciliation §10). The projection is
   a **lossy view**: it collapses empty-vs-absent to `NA`, drops delimiter state,
   and is shaped by presentation dials (P2.2 §4). Field-level equality between
   two rows' projections is **not** evidence of standard conformance and may not
   be counted, aggregated, or reported as such. Component fixtures remain valid
   for **characterization and regression** (that shipped output is stable), never
   for a standards or state-preservation gate (S1-F10: "Snapshot equality alone
   must not satisfy a standards or state-preservation gate").

3. **Historical projection-based claims are preserved as historical evidence
   only.** Every pre-v3 conformance claim built on component projections (the
   `aligned` self-consistency bucket, `accept`-only admission cells, hand-derived
   `clean_url` string checks; S8 conformance matrix, S1-F10) is **retained
   unaltered as historical evidence** and **retired as a live oracle**. It is not
   deleted, not rewritten as current architecture, and not cited forward as a
   standing conformance guarantee (§5 required disposition). Any live v3 claim
   that reuses such a fixture must re-derive its expected value from an
   independent oracle (§2) against the FSSS, not inherit the projection's verdict.

### 2. Oracle policy — every oracle is labeled by authority and claim boundary

Per G4 ("Oracles are labeled by authority and claim boundary"), v3 recognizes a
closed taxonomy of oracle **authorities**. Each oracle instance (fixture set,
grammar seam, differential comparator) carries a machine-readable **authority
label** and an explicit **claim boundary** — what it *can* certify and what it
*cannot*. The parity-vs-conformance distinction is normative: a **parity** oracle
certifies *agreement with another implementation*, never *standard conformance*.

| Authority label | What it IS | CAN certify | CANNOT certify (claim boundary) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `whatwg-wpt` | Imported WHATWG URL Standard / Web Platform Tests rows, pinned to an immutable upstream revision + hash | WHATWG serialization/parsing conformance for the represented WPT cases, asserted against the FSSS `whatwg` output | RFC 3986 behavior; unrepresented cases; performance; API shape; "100% WHATWG conformance" beyond imported rows |
| `rfc3986-grammar` | The RFC 3986 ABNF seam (`rfc3986-abnf.csv`), audited and repaired (RURL-nknytzxz) | Independent syntax **acceptance/rejection** for the represented RFC 3986 productions | Full serialization; WHATWG algorithms; Unicode/IRI behavior; universal RFC conformance |
| `libcurl-parity` | Differential comparison against `curl_parse_url()` output | **Agreement/divergence** with the libcurl backend (a compatibility fact) | Standard conformance of either side; it is a **parity ledger**, never a conformance oracle (ADR 0012 treats parity as a compatibility layer, not a standard oracle) |
| `browser-parity` | Differential comparison against a browser's observed fix-up behavior | **Agreement/divergence** with modeled browser repair | Standard conformance; browsers repair beyond both standards |
| `self-metamorphic` | Internal invariants over canonical state: parse→serialize→parse identity, format-non-authority, clean loss-budget, mutation preservation of untouched components, scalar/vector parity, cache transparency (S1-F10) | **Structural/relational** properties of the implementation against itself | Any external standard conformance; self-consistency is characterization, **not** a standards claim (S8 High 6: the `aligned` bucket "must not be counted as conformance coverage") |

Rules binding the taxonomy:

1. **No parity oracle certifies conformance.** `libcurl-parity` and
   `browser-parity` produce *ledgers of agreement*, reported separately from
   conformance. A row where two profiles or two backends agree is **not**
   conformance evidence (S8 High 6; the WPT-into-RFC contamination that produced
   false-green results, `NEWS.md:244-275`, is why authority and claim boundary
   are mandatory, not advisory).
2. **The defensible headline number is a labeled count, not a parity probe.**
   Conformance is reported as *N conforming / M documented deviations against
   `<authority>` `<standard>`*. The one **already-audited** such figure is
   **158 conforming / 99 documented deviations against `rfc3986-grammar`** — an
   **RFC 3986 syntax acceptance/rejection** count over the **257-row audited
   fixture** (81 rejects-what-RFC-admits, 18 accepts-what-RFC-forbids;
   `NEWS.md:271-274`, RURL-nknytzxz), **not** a "100%" parity-probe result and
   **not** the FSSS full-string conformance headline (which is per §1 asserted on
   `serialize_url()` and whose suites are still to be built — see Consequences).
   It is a `rfc3986-grammar`-authority *acceptance* count; per §2's own table
   that authority **cannot certify full serialization**. Every headline states
   its authority + standard + fixture scope this way; deviations carry an
   owner/status/expiry and a v3 disposition, and a skipped-deviation row is never
   silently folded into a conformance total.
3. **Every imported oracle pins provenance.** Immutable upstream project /
   revision / path, retrieval date, license, raw-source hash, import/generation
   command, transformed-fixture hash, standard/version/section, claim kind, and
   applicability selector (S8 §Conformance matrix; Oracle integrity gate 9).

### 3. Claim-provenance and coverage requirement (sets up G4 executable evidence)

1. **Every normative claim maps to a labeled oracle + fixtures.** No v3 normative
   claim (conformance, migration, or property) graduates without a traceable edge
   to (a) one authority label from §2, and (b) a fixture set with an independent
   expected value. This is the executable-evidence spine G4 requires ("Every
   normative claim maps to tests, fixtures, benchmarks, or a documented manual
   verification with exact commands").
2. **Positive AND negative coverage.** Conformance evidence must exercise both
   acceptance and rejection / lossy-change paths over the G4 property families:
   **full-string**, **state**, **vector**, **mutation**, **join**, **cache**,
   **determinism**, and **migration** (G4). A fixture family with only positive
   rows does not satisfy the claim.
3. **Metamorphic assertions over canonical state are required, not optional.**
   Per S1-F10 the `self-metamorphic` oracle must assert, at minimum:
   parse→serialize→parse equivalence, format non-authority (display output never
   re-enters serialization), clean loss-budget, mutation preservation of
   untouched components, scalar/vector parity, and cache transparency. Snapshot
   equality alone never discharges a standards or state-preservation claim.
4. **Deferred to siblings (referenced, not decided).** The **determinism**
   property's failing-gate tolerance and approved-exception mechanism is **P5.2**
   (C-09) — this record only requires that determinism *have* positive/negative
   coverage and a labeled oracle, and defers the pass/fail tolerance to P5.2. The
   **cache**-transparency budget and the **performance** budgets are P5.1's; the
   **migration** completeness ledger is the P-tier migration record's. P5.3 fixes
   *that* each property carries a labeled oracle and provenance; it does not fix
   each property's numeric budget.

## Rationale

- **Reconciliation §5 C-12 (verbatim required disposition).** "Preserve them as
  historical evidence only; rebuild claims from full state and full-string
  outputs." §1 is the literal rebuild: claims stand on the FSSS full string and
  parse→serialize→parse identity (full state), and projection-based history is
  retained-but-retired.
- **Reconciliation §4 RCON-10.** The finding is that "conformance, determinism,
  migration, and release claims are not executable": the protocol "permits
  examples, component projections, and artifact collection without requiring
  claim-level provenance or failing acceptance gates." The required amendment
  names "a source-claim register; oracle-classified and metamorphic fixtures;
  full-string conformance assertions; … claim-level provenance". §1 supplies
  full-string assertions, §2 supplies oracle classification, §3 supplies
  claim-level provenance. RCON-10's open owner decisions include "conformance
  claim granularity; oracle authority and snapshot policy" — settled here at the
  claim/oracle layer (release-line and CRAN scope stay with P0.4/C-10;
  determinism tolerance with P5.2/C-09).
- **Reconciliation §4 RCON-09.** "Vectorization, performance, caches … are not
  measurable gates." §3's requirement that the **vector** and **cache** property
  families carry positive/negative coverage and a labeled oracle is the
  claim-side hook into RCON-09; the numeric budgets themselves are P5.1.
- **Reconciliation §7 G4.** "Oracles are labeled by authority and claim
  boundary" and "Full-string, state, vector, mutation, join, cache, determinism,
  and migration properties have positive and negative coverage." §2 and §3
  implement G4 verbatim; this record is the policy G4's executable evidence
  discharges against.
- **Reconciliation §8 P5.** The P5 queue names "Oracle policy, determinism
  tolerance, migration window, and graduation gates." P5.3 takes **oracle
  policy** (and the conformance-claim-granularity slice of it); determinism
  tolerance is P5.2, migration window is the migration record, graduation gates
  are G4/G5.
- **Frozen evidence S8 (`evidence/S8-performance-curl-conformance-migration.md`).**
  The conformance/oracle matrix (§Conformance and oracle evidence matrix) is the
  primary source for §2: it separates normative RFC grammar, external adversarial
  vectors, the deviation ledger, the `aligned` self-consistency bucket ("must not
  be counted as conformance coverage"), hand-derived fixtures, characterization
  snapshots, and the historical parser comparator — each with an explicit "what
  it cannot support" column, which is exactly the claim-boundary field of §2.
  S8 High 6 ("conformance provenance and claim scope are not mandatory enough to
  prevent a known failure mode") and the documented WPT-into-RFC contamination
  (`NEWS.md:244-275`) are why the parity-vs-conformance rule (§2.1) is binding.
  S8's Oracle integrity gate 9 supplies the provenance field list (§2.3).
- **Frozen evidence S1-F10
  (`evidence/S1-object-state-vector.md` §S1-F10).** "Current tests are useful
  characterization, but the protocol does not mandate oracle classification plus
  metamorphic state assertions." Its concrete correction — an oracle label per
  fixture (`v2-characterization`, `accepted-ADR contract`, `external
  conformance`, `proposed-v3`, `regression`) plus metamorphic assertions
  (parse→serialize→parse equivalence, format non-authority, clean loss-budget,
  mutation preservation, scalar/vector parity, cache transparency) — is the
  direct source of the `self-metamorphic` authority (§2) and §3.3. Its
  "Structural round-trip matrix" and "Oracle register" required artifacts
  (S1-F10 table) are the fixture families §3 requires.
- **P2.2 (this branch).** §4 of P2.2 delivers the FSSS and states explicitly that
  "the claim/oracle policy itself … is the scope of sibling record P5.3, which
  depends on this record's FSSS definition." This record is that dependent, and
  cites P2.2's surface without redefining it.

## Consequences

- **A conformance claim now has a fixed shape.** Every v3 conformance claim names
  a standard, a labeled oracle, an input/fixture set, and a comparison mode, and
  is asserted against the FSSS full string (or parse→serialize→parse identity).
  The bare phrase "rurl is WHATWG-conformant" is not a v3 claim; "N conforming /
  M documented deviations against `whatwg-wpt` rev X, asserted on
  `serialize_url(x, standard='whatwg')`" is.
- **The defensible headline number is a labeled count.** Reporting standardizes on
  *conforming / documented-deviation* counts per authority+standard (the audited
  158/99 is specifically an **`rfc3986-grammar` acceptance** count over the
  257-row fixture, `NEWS.md:271-274` — not the FSSS conformance headline), never
  a "100% parity" probe. Parity ledgers (libcurl, browser) are reported in a
  separate column and never summed into a conformance total.
- **The projection stops being an oracle.** Component-field equality is confined
  to characterization/regression. Downstream code or docs that (mis)cite field
  equality as conformance is legacy to migrate, not evidence the projection is an
  oracle — mirroring how P2.2 removes the excuse for `clean_url`-as-serializer.
- **G4 is unblocked on the oracle/claim axis.** The executable-evidence gate can
  require, per normative claim, an authority label and a positive+negative
  fixture family across the eight property groups; the traceability map has a
  policy to validate against.
- **New evidence to build.** A full-string WHATWG conformance suite and a
  full-string RFC 3986 suite (both on the FSSS, both with pinned provenance), a
  `self-metamorphic` property suite (parse→serialize→parse and the S1-F10
  metamorphic set), and separated parity ledgers. The existing characterization
  snapshots stay, re-labeled `v2-characterization`/`regression`.
- **Sibling coupling.** P5.2 must supply the determinism tolerance the
  determinism property's gate uses; P5.1 the cache/perf budgets; P0.4 the release
  line. P5.3's coverage requirement *names* these property families but leaves
  their pass/fail thresholds to those records — an accepted P5.3 does not
  pre-empt them.

## Open questions for the owner

1. **Disposition type for the C-12 claim/oracle half.** §1–§3 read most naturally
   as **COMPATIBILITY-ONLY** for the historical claims (retained as evidence,
   retired as oracles) plus a forward **ACCEPTED** policy for the FSSS-based
   claims. *Recommendation:* record C-12's claim/oracle half as
   **COMPATIBILITY-ONLY** (historical preservation) with the new claim/oracle
   policy accepted forward. **Please confirm the typed disposition.**
2. **Closed vs open oracle taxonomy.** §2 lists five authorities
   (`whatwg-wpt`, `rfc3986-grammar`, `libcurl-parity`, `browser-parity`,
   `self-metamorphic`). Is this the **closed** set for v3, or should a sixth
   (e.g. an IDNA/UTS-46 conformance authority for host processing, or an
   `email`/RFC 5321-5322 authority) be reserved now? *Recommendation:* treat the
   five as closed for the URL-serialization claim surface and reserve host/email
   conformance authorities to the P4 host/scheme records so they inherit this
   labeling discipline rather than duplicating it.
3. **Headline-number governance.** Should the **158 conforming / 99 documented
   deviations** figure be pinned as a versioned claim (per FSSS + per pinned WPT
   revision) that a gate re-computes, or reported as current-run output only?
   *Recommendation:* make it a **gate-recomputed, provenance-pinned** number so
   the headline cannot drift from the fixtures, deferring the exact threshold
   (may it decrease? by how much?) to G4/G5.
4. **Deviation lifecycle authority.** Each documented deviation needs an
   owner/status/expiry and a v3 disposition (§2.3; S8 High 6). Is that lifecycle
   owned here (P5.3), in the per-fixture oracle register, or in the migration
   ledger? *Recommendation:* own the *schema* here (label + boundary + provenance
   + deviation status fields) and the *per-row lifecycle* in the oracle register,
   so P5.3 stays policy and the register stays data.
5. **Parity-ledger retention.** §2.1 keeps `libcurl-parity` as a reported-separate
   ledger. Once curl is removed (RCON-09 / P5.1 curl-removal sequence), does the
   libcurl parity ledger become **historical evidence only** (like the projection
   claims), or is it retained as a frozen compatibility snapshot? *Recommendation:*
   freeze it as historical evidence at curl-removal, matching §1.3's treatment of
   retired oracles.
